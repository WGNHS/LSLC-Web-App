History

For 40 years, beginning in 1882, the Lake Superior Division of the USGS in Madison, Wisconsin, documented the geology of the Lake Superior area. The project primarily covered Wisconsin, Minnesota, Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, and Ontario. Over the course of the project, the group produced a number of publications on the region’s Precambrian geology. R.D. Irving, the first director of the division, did pioneering work in the field of microscopic petrography and was the first to use thin sections in a large-scale survey.
